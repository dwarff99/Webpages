ABOUT

FIRST OPENED.

On October 21, 2009

Mumbai is known to be the city that never sleeps; its vibe is pulsating in its cosmopolitan nature and it’s always craving for more space for its burgeoning population. Luckily for us, exotic Dutch dancer Mata Hari has come to town in all her grandeur and opulence, changing the way we party.
For long now the city has been missing a night spot that exudes the charm of a long forgotten era. Matahaari is a tribute to the iconic past of a courtesan and secret agent, as well as a giant leap forward into the 21st century of entertainment.
This grand 7,500 sq feet nightclub housed in Atria Mall, Worli is the city’s hottest and newest party destination.

THE BEST NIGHT CLUB EVER.

Owned by renowned hotelier B.R. Shetty, Managing Director B & R Leisure Pvt. Ltd. with over 30 years in the business & fashion designer Chanchal Shetty in partnership with acclaimed Architect Bobby Mukherji of Bobby Mukherji & Associates. Matahaari is here to stay and seduce you with her charms.
The firm helmed by Director, Bobby Mukherji & Associates, Paulomi Shah, an experienced design whiz when it comes to luxury hospitality spaces around the world has passionately conceptualised the design narrative of Matahaari. Together, they have personified the infamous dancer through art and architecture.
Noted food consultant and gourmand Nikhil Merchant has conceptualised an extensive food and beverage menu keeping in sync with the theme and succeeds in bringing to life Matahaari and her inexhaustible appetite for all things luxurious. In keeping with her Asian influences, Matahaari’s cuisine spans Chinese, Japanese and Thai, along with Indian dishes. The 70+ delicacies is executed by their corporate chef Shailendra Bhandari with a customizable banquet menu selection for private events. Five signature cocktails, with names synonymous to the dancer are on the cards, especially the firecracker of a cocktail ‘Femme Fatale’.
With much delight and decadence promised at every step of the way, we look forward to welcoming you to Matahaari for a night of revelry
